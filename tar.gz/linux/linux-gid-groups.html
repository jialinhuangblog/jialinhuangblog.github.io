# My user

# Query the members of group=staff, but only root is found
dscl . -read /Groups/staff GroupMembership
# GroupMembership: root

So, why is it that you clearly belong to the staff group, but you can't see yourself in the group's member list?

This is similar to being a VIP member at a restaurant. You have a physical membership card, so you're definitely a member. But if the waiter only checks the reservation book, they might not find your name. However, showing your card would prove your membership.

Imagine that gid is the place you belong to, called staff, but the externally displayed groups don't show you as a staff member.

The difference between gid=20(staff) and groups=20(staff) is this: gid is your affiliation, while groups are the places you can go, including the subsequent 12, 61... which are all places you can access.

Coincidentally, one of the places you can go is your official affiliation. That's why you see both gid=20 and groups=20.

You directly belong to the staff group as your Primary Group, which is set through PrimaryGroupID, not through GroupMembership.

dscl . -read /Users/jialinhuang PrimaryGroupID
# PrimaryGroupID: 20


dscl . -read /Groups/staff GroupMembership PrimaryGroupID NestedGroups
# GroupMembership: root
# PrimaryGroupID: 20
# No such key: NestedGroups
